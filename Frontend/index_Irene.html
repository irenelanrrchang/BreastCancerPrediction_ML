The Breast Cancer Machine Learning Prediction

Goal: Increasing precision rate of BCa diagnosis

About Project

Breast cancer is the second most diagnosed cancer in women, affecting one in every eight women in the U.S and it is second to lung cancer as a cause of death. Women whose breast cancer is detected at an early stage have a 93 percent or higher survival rate in the first five years. Over 50% of US breast cancer patients are working-aged women. Thus, if the accuracy of early detection rate is improved, it will decrease the morbidity, mortality, health economic burden and the impact on their employers and families.

Digital lab applied 6 machine learning models to predict breast cancer and here are our breif introductions and logics we followed to achieved the results:

  • Models: Support Vector Machine (SVM), Decision Tree (C4.5), Naive Bayes (NB) and k Nearest Neighbors (k-NN), random forest, neural network
  • Team: Irene, Cong, Boren, Mike
  • Technology: Python Keras, scikit-learn, Pandas, Matplotlib | Javascript, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap
  • Project Github Link: Breast Cancer Machine Learning Prediction Project
